Key production
team

Our film is the result of collaboration between emerging filmmakers from the American Film Institute Conservatory. Each member of our crew brings a unique artistic voice and professional dedication.

Writer, Producer

Lily Malm

Lily Malm is a Swedish-born, LA-based screenwriter and AFI Fellow. Her work ranges from politically tinged dramas and thrillers to lesbian dramedies with a focus on capturing the nuance of the queer female experience. Her feature script “Scent of Marigold”, co-written by Laila Matuk, was selected as an American Zoetrope finalist by Francis Ford Coppola, made it into PAGE Awards, Atlanta Film Festival, ScreenCraft, Launch Pad, San Francisco International Film Festival, Final Draft’s Big Break, Josephson Entertainment Fellowship, among others, won for Best Drama at Big Apple Film Festival and became a finalist in the Austin Film Festival where it was picked up and optioned, earning her an “Alien with Extraordinary Abilities” artist visa.

lmalm@afi.edu

Writer

Tickwanya Jones

Tickwanya Jones is a versatile creative based in Atlanta and LA, originally from Richmond, VA. A proud Berklee College of Music graduate, she went from working in labels and publishing to performance, collaborating as a background vocalist with artists like Lizzo, Childish Gambino, and Kanye West. Her voiceover credits include brands like L.O.L. Surprise, Nickelodeon, MTV, and Mattel. Tickwanya has also appeared in commercials for Burger King and on stage at venues like Pasadena Playhouse. During the pandemic, she studied screenwriting at the UCLA Extension, adding writing to her artistic repertoire. Jones is currently pursing an MFA in Writing for Film and Television at Emerson College.

tickwanya.writes@gmail.com

Director

S. S. Ayers

Shakira “Stori” Ayers is an actor, director and co-founder of [RARE] Lotus Productions. She was an original cast member and producer of Dominique Morisseau’s “Blood at the Root,” winner of the Graham F. Smith Peace Foundation Prize for its promotion of human rights. She appeared in the TBS series “The Last O.G” and in the Broadway production of “Home.” As a director, Ayers won the Antonyo Award for Best Direction (Off- Broadway) and was nominated for Outstanding Direction by the Drama League. Ayers holds a B.A. from Mary Baldwin University and an MFA in acting from Penn State University. Now an AFI Fellow, she has a passion for stories that challenge social norms, ignite a spirit of activism and inspire future generations.

sayers@afi.edu

Producer

Cierra Hendrix

Cierra Hendrix is a multi-hyphenate storyteller. With roots in live television, radio and podcast hosting, she has built a diverse creative portfolio as a Producer, director, and photographer. A graduate of Norfolk State University and currently earning her MFA in producing at the American Film Institute, Cierra has produced several narrative short films, created live broadcasted talk shows, and achieved award-winning short content through programs like the Nate Parker Foundation. Her work blends technical skill with emotional depth, centering powerful stories that reflect and uplift underrepresented voices.

chendrix@afi.edu

Editor

April Yang

Xinzhen “April" Yang is an AFI Editing Fellow, post-production specialist, and graduate of NYU Shanghai with a Bachelor of Science in Interactive Media Art and Film Production. With a background in both computer science and filmmaking, she brings both coding skills and a creative vision to the editing room. Her short psychological thriller “Corroded Finger” was selected at the 2024 Toronto International Nollywood Film Festival, qualifying for the Canadian Screen Awards. April has worked as an editing assistant in several places, such as Sundance Institute and Hearst Media. Her work includes award-winning narrative shorts (TINFF, Stockholm City Film Festival), features, documentaries, commercials, AI films and collaborations with major productions including Jackie Chan's “Panda Plan.”

xyang2@afi.edu

Director of Photography

Anna Mendes

Anna Mendes Caldeira Rathsam is a Brazilian cinematographer and AFI Fellow based in LA. Her work ranges from ad campaigns for Fortune 500 companies like Uber Eats, Twitter, and Google to narrative films. A narrative short she DP’d called “They Come From the Sky” screened at the American Cinematheque's PROOF Film Festival and the New York Latino Film Festival, presented by Warner Bros. Discovery. It was also featured on the popular sci-fi DUST channel, drawing over 200,000 views. In 2023, she was selected from over 1,500 applicants as one of four cinematographers in Color Creative's Find Your People Program. Through the program, she worked as the DP on the short film just one, which premiered at UTA and screened at the Pan African Film Festival, with Issa Rae serving as executive producer.

amendescaldeirarathsam@afi.edu

Production Designer

Emily Ma

Emily Ma is a production designer based in Los Angeles. She is currently earning her M.F.A. in Production Design from the American Film Institute Conservatory and holds dual degrees in Film Production (B.S.) and Psychology (B.A.) from Boston University. Many of Emily’s works have been recognized by festivals such as South by Southwest (SXSW) and the Directors Guild of America (DGA). As an artist, Emily aims to bring voice to the voiceless and is always challenging herself with more creative ways of storytelling.

ema@afi.edu

Producer

Nicole Lasso Polo

Nicole Lasso Polo is a Panamanian filmmaker based in LA who has written, directed and produced multiple projects, some of which have been officially selected and screened at various film festivals. She is a recent graduate of the University of Central Florida, where she focused on Narrative Production. One of the projects she produced, “To Catch Lightning,” screened at the Florida Film Festival. Additionally, her film “Branches” was nominated for Best Women Short at Indie Shorts Fest. Currently, she's a Producing Fellow the American Film Institute Conservatory.

nlasso@afi.edu
